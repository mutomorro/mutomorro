Systemic patterns beneath the symptoms
Manager time squeeze
Managers carrying operational load that leaves no space for people leadership. Development, recognition and communication all suffer from the same root cause.
Affects 3 touchpoints
Information flows around, not through
Important decisions reach teams through informal channels before official ones. Creates a trust gap that surveys do not capture.
Erodes trust
Experience gap widens with distance from centre
Senior leaders score experience 20+ points higher than frontline teams. The organisation designed for itself, not for the people delivering services.
Structural pattern
Recognition is personal, not systemic
Where recognition happens, it depends on individual managers, not organisational practices. Good managers create pockets of great experience. The rest drift.
Design opportunity
The experience is better than surveys suggest in some places, worse in others
The biggest insight is not the scores but the patterns. Four systemic issues explain most of the experience gaps. Addressing these will shift outcomes across multiple touchpoints simultaneously.
