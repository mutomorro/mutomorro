Structural bottleneck patterns
Practice silos block cross-practice delivery
The structure was designed for single-practice projects. Most work now spans practices. Teams work around the structure, not through it.
→ Average 3-week delay assembling cross-practice teams
Partner bottleneck on decisions
Four partners each handling 40+ decisions per week that could be made at senior associate level. Capacity fully consumed.
→ Partners spending 60% of time on operational decisions, 40% on strategic
Knowledge locked in individuals
No structural mechanism for sharing expertise across practices. Three departures in the last year caused significant client impact.
→ Client satisfaction dropped 12 points after each departure
Growth constrained by current design
The firm has outgrown its structure. What worked for 80 people does not work for 220. The design needs to evolve, not just stretch.
→ Revenue growth slowing despite strong demand
