Structural strain points
Community Services directorate no longer fits
Services have evolved but the structure has not. Teams doing related work sit in different directorates. Collaboration requires heroic effort from individuals.
→ 40% of managers report cross-directorate friction weekly
Duplicate management layers slowing decisions
Two layers of management between front-line teams and directors. Decisions that should take hours take weeks. Staff frustrated.
→ Average decision time for routine matters: 11 days
Middle management carrying unsustainable load
Team leaders managing operational delivery and change simultaneously. Burnout risk is real. Several already expressing intent to leave.
→ 6 of 14 team leaders at flight risk
Resident-facing services fragmented
Residents interact with 3-4 different teams for related needs. No single point of ownership. Experience is inconsistent.
→ Resident satisfaction: 52%. Council average: 64%
Previous restructure trauma still present
Last restructure (2021) was poorly handled. Staff carry mistrust. This restructure needs to be different - or it will deepen the damage.
→ 67% of staff say "restructure" makes them anxious
